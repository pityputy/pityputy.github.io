The Book of Cheese

Gilvert

1 - The First Teaching

1For whom the bells toll, for whom a thousand ships have sailed out to sea. For our protector, caretaker, guardian and wise leader, The Great Feta. 2May thy wisdom guide us through the winding path called life.

3As a young lad I believed in fairy tales. But they were more real than reality sometimes. 4I lived in a small town, with a name not worthy of mentioning in such a sacred book. 5One day, exactly on the forty-seventh of Besiry I walked out to the fields where I felt a calling I never felt before. 6The calling turned my head to the East where laid the Boobobaga Mountains. Peaks so high you could see through the Hole of the Doughnut standing atop.

7With determination I never felt before I trod through the mud. My consciousness has left my body. 8I saw a weary traveller boy bewitched by some supernatural spirit whose intentions were unbeknownst to him like they were to anybody watching. 9Nobody stood there though, but for the mole-people who lived right under the town. 10The boy didn’t even look at them as he walked by. 11The calling didn't care about others’ opinion.

12I was the boy. The boy was me, yet I wasn’t the one in charge. It was the One in charge. 13Or One of the Ones. I didn’t know back then.

14I marched for days without any food or water, my Guide gave me strength. 16If thy will is strong, so is thy body and mind. said the Calling one day.

17On the eighth day of my voyage I reached the mountains. I climbed and climbed up the rocky walls, just as the Voice told me. 18By the eleventh morning I greeted the rising sun standing on the mountainside looking down on the Doughnut below.

18When I turned I saw beauty resting at the top. A grand piece of cheese that had a calming presence. 19Its wisdom beaming out into the air filling all the eager listeners like me with courage to find It.

20I sense thou art one of the curious ones. said Its voice in my head. I have all the answers to thy questions. Thou just hast to ask.

21How would I ask? I said out loud. If even I, myself don’t know the questions?

22Is that a question? Do thou wantest me to answer that?

23I don’t know.

24Think again. If thou art asking yourself that question thou art in the wrong place. Come back when thou knowest what wantest to ask.

25I walked away but without shame. I knew I would come back, and this was only the first lesson. 26I found my new Master.
