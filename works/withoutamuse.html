Without a Muse

What's a love poem without a muse?
Would it be a sad poet's last resort?
The Universe has a sense of humour
That no man can grasp, let alone get
Yet I'm here writing a love poem
To a muse of no name and god
To a muse of no flesh and blood
But a thin fibre of thoughts
And now she flies, among the clouds
And I fall among the leaves
And the trees look happy from down here
As she flies further, with each breath I take
Her angel wings cut through the air
And I sink deeper into the mud
I came from dust, and now I return
To the earth that I always loved
